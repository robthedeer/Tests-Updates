Nutrition Guide

Expert information to help you build strength through proper nutrition

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Registered Dietitian & Nutrition Specialist

"Building strength requires a balanced approach to nutrition. Focus on getting adequate protein for muscle repair, complex carbohydrates for sustained energy, and healthy fats for hormone production. Don't neglect micronutrients - they're essential for all bodily functions."

Macronutrients

Protein

Essential for muscle repair and growth. Aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight daily.

Carbohydrates

Primary energy source. Focus on complex carbs like whole grains, vegetables, and legumes.

Fats

Necessary for hormone production and nutrient absorption. Prioritize unsaturated fats.

Micronutrients

Vitamins

Essential for energy production, immune function, and bone health. Focus on:

  • Vitamin D: Bone health and immunity
  • B Vitamins: Energy metabolism
  • Vitamin C: Antioxidant and collagen production

Minerals

Crucial for muscle function and oxygen transport:

  • Iron: Oxygen transport in blood
  • Calcium: Bone health and muscle function
  • Magnesium: Energy production and muscle relaxation
Hydration

Water Intake

Adequate hydration is crucial for physical performance and recovery. Aim for:

  • 2.5-3.5 liters per day for men
  • 2.0-2.7 liters per day for women

Increase intake during exercise and in hot environments.

Electrolytes

Essential for nerve function and fluid balance. Include sodium, potassium, and magnesium in your diet.

Meal Timing

Pre-Workout Nutrition

Consume a balanced meal 2-3 hours before exercise containing carbs and protein.

Post-Workout Recovery

Within 30-60 minutes after exercise, consume protein and carbs to aid recovery.

Frequency

Eating every 3-4 hours helps maintain energy levels and supports muscle growth.
